How It Works

zk_faucet uses zero-knowledge storage proofs to verify you hold a minimum ETH balance on mainnet without revealing your identity. The proof is generated client-side and verified off-chain, ensuring your mainnet address is never linked to your testnet claim.

Claim Flow

1. Connect your wallet and sign the epoch message
2. A ZK proof is generated proving your balance exceeds the minimum
3. The proof includes a nullifier derived from your public key and the current epoch
4. The server verifies the proof and sends testnet ETH to your chosen address
5. The nullifier prevents double-claiming within the same epoch

Privacy

Your mainnet identity is never revealed to the faucet server. The ZK proof only demonstrates that some address holds a sufficient balance — the server never learns which address that is. The nullifier is derived from your public key and the epoch, preventing double-claims without linking to your identity.
